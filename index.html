You could say that my father is dying. He is 47 years old, and just like the rest of us he isnâ€™t getting any younger. You could actually say that my mother, sister, brother-in-law, boyfriend, coworkers, acquaintances, schoolmates, strangers, and every other organism in existence is dying. As we grow older each day, we are one day closer to the end of our life.


I came to this realization a few years back. My last living grandparent had recently passed away, and I had a sort of epiphany.


I realized life is short, and decided that I would only pursue things in life that made me happy.


I was already in college at that point, attending the local community college because it was more cost-effective than sending me to a four-year university. After my grandma died, I started thinking about what really made me happy. And then I realized, one of the happiest times in my life was when I was on yearbook staff in high school.


I know it might sound corny, but I truly enjoyed being in yearbook. I made the staff my sophomore year and was editor-in-chief my senior year. I loved making layouts in InDesign, not just because I was good at it, but because I enjoyed the notion that people would enjoy looking at what I created.


Then I realized what would make me happy: a career in journalism with a focus in design.


I graduated from Hillsborough Community College Spring 2012, and that fall I finally made my way to the University of Florida College of Journalism and Communication.


Since arriving at UF I have worked at the Independent Florida Alligator, Our Town Magazine, WUFT News, The Ledger, Orange and Blue Magazine and have been actively involved in the Society of Professional Journalists.


Over the past year and a half I have learned more about print design, but more importantly I learned about web design. I was hooked. I hope to one day have the opportunity to incorporate my love of design, music and pop culture in a professional and creative environment. It's what makes me happy, and I won't let anything get in the way of that.
