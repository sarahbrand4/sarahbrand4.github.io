ABOUT

Hello, my name is Sarah Brand. You killed my father, prepare to die- oh wait, my father's not dead yet.


You could say that my father is dying. He is 47 years old, just like the rest of us he isnâ€™t getting any younger. You could actually say that my mother, sister, brother-in-law, boyfriend, coworkers, acquaintances, schoolmates, strangers, and every other organism in existence is dying. As we grow older each day, we are one day closer to the end of our life.


I came to this realization a few years back. My last living grandparent had recently passed away, and I had a sort of epiphany.


I came to the decision that I would never do anything that didn't make me happy.


I was already in college at that point, attending the local community college because it was more cost-effective than sending me to a four-year university. After my grandma died, I started thinking about what really made me happy. And then I realized, one of the happiest times in my life was when I was on yearbook staff in high school.


I know it might sound corny, but I truly enjoyed being in yearbook. I made the staff my sophomore year and was editor-in-chief my senior year. I loved making layouts in InDesign, not just because I was good at it, but because I enjoyed the notion that people would like looking at what I created.


And in that moment of realization, I knew what would make me happy : a career in journalism.


I graduated from Hillsborough Community College Spring 2012, and that fall I finally made my way to the University of Florida College of Journalism and Communication, where I knew my journalism career could really start.


Since arriving at UF I have worked at the Independent Florida Alligator, Our Town Magazine, WUFT News, The Ledger and have actively been involved in the Society of Professional Journalists.


Through my experiences I found a passion for good design and pop culture. I love well written TV shows and music. I hope to one day have the opportunity to incorporate my love of design, pop culture and entertainment in a professional and creative environment. It's what makes me happy, and I won't let anything get in the way of that.
